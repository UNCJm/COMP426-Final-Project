HANGMAN

SETTING UP THE GAME

You must have node.js installed. Open the folder with Visual Studio Code. Open the terminal in VSC, and type in these lines of code sequentially:

cd hangman_backend
npm install
npm start

(It is unnecessary to run npm install after the first time)

Afterwards, open a browser of your choosing and enter this address:

http://localhost:3000/hangman.html

Any time in game, you can click on the 'HANGMAN' logo to return to the title screen and save your high score.

BASIC RULES

The game functions as your normal game of Hangman, and you gain a single point for every word you complete, but there's a few differences.

One, when you start a new word, a few letters are already revealed to you. The number of letters revealed depends on how many unique letters a word has. In that sense, the game is easier, but there's a reason for this.

Second... well, there are more than 2 differences, but I'll start with Curses.

CURSES

You will notice a purple bar running below the Hangman display. This is the Curse Meter.

Whenever the Curse Meter fills up, a Curse latches upon a letter that is:
1) Not a letter you've currently guessed this round.
2) Not a CORRECT letter for this round's word.

When you guess a letter attached with a Curse, it will be shown with a purple color, and counts as 2 wrong guesses instead of 1.

You can't see which letters have a Curse, but you can see how many Curses are in the current pool.

How Curses can be removed from the pool:
1) A single Curse gets removed every time you complete a word.
2) When you trigger a Curse, it gets removed from the pool.
3) When you start a new round, existing Curses that are actually in the new word are removed from the pool.
4) After every fifth round, ALL Curses are removed from the pool.
5) When you finish a word, the Curse Meter resets. This doesn't remove a Curse, but technically it prevents one.

SUBJECTS

Most of the time, the Hangman figure will look normal as shown above, with the Subject ID of 'johndoe'. However, that's not always the case.

With a 20% chance, you may encounter an 'Elite' Subject, with additional effects that will make your life harder for that round, or may flip game mechanics on its head.

Outside of that 20% chance, every FIFTH round are guaranteed to be an Elite. There are 6 Elites in total.

As you'd recall, every fifth round will also cleanse all Curses, which means if you manage to beat the Elite, you get all Curses cleansed, but it also means the moment when you have to fight one is when Curses have accumulated the most.

THE GOAL OF THE GAME

You can simply play indefinitely until you lose, but the breakpoint I intended for people to aim for is 30 points, and here's why:

Every TENTH round are guaranteed to be a 'Boss' Subject. They are SIGNIFICANTLY deadlier than an Elite, and there are 3 in total.

Bosses will never appear outside of every tenth round, and will not reappear onwards until you've defeated all of them.

This means that if you've managed a high score of 30 or higher, you've achieved the feat of defeating all 3 Bosses in a single run, which, believe me, is harder than it sounds.

If you've managed to do so, there's a surprise waiting for you on the title screen.
